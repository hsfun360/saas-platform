# My Dashboard

> **Where:** the **My Dashboard** item at the top of the side menu (or the first icon in the bottom bar on a phone).
> **Who can use it:** every signed-in user; the page shows only screens your own role can open.

## What this option is for

My Dashboard is your personal home page.
It is the same page whichever system you are working in, and it is the first place you land to start your day.
It gathers three things in one view: **Quick access** (screens you pinned with the star), **Continue where you left off** (screens you visited recently), and **Help & guides** (screens that have a built-in user guide).
Your pinned screens are saved to your account, so the same Quick access follows you on your office computer, laptop and phone.

## The screen at a glance

[Screenshot: My Dashboard with pinned screens in two system groups]

- A greeting with your name, your company and your role.
- **Quick access** - your pinned screens as cards, grouped under a heading per system (for example SYSTEM ADMINISTRATION, GOLF MANAGEMENT).
  Each heading shows the system icon, a count of pinned screens inside, and an arrow to fold the group.
  The eye icon beside the "Quick access" title hides or shows the whole section.
  The **Manage** button opens a window to reorder or remove your pins.
- **Continue where you left off** - buttons for the screens you opened most recently, across all systems.
  Click one to jump straight back in.
- **Help & guides** - the screens that have a built-in user guide.
  Open one of them and use the book icon in the top bar to read its guide.

## Common tasks

### Pin a screen to Quick access

1. Open any screen from the side menu.
2. Click the **star** next to the screen title.
   The star fills in to show the screen is pinned.
3. The screen now appears on My Dashboard under its system's heading.

To unpin, click the filled star again on that screen, or click the small star on the card in Quick access.

### Hide or show the Quick access section

- Click the **eye** icon beside the "Quick access" title.
  An open eye means the section is shown; a crossed eye means it is folded away to just its title.
- The system remembers your choice on this computer.

### Fold or unfold a system group

- Click a system heading (or its arrow) to fold that group's cards away; click again to unfold.
- The count on the heading always tells you how many screens are pinned inside, even while folded.
- The system remembers which groups you folded on this computer.

### Reorder or remove your pins

[Screenshot: Manage quick access window with two system blocks]

1. Click **Manage** beside the "Quick access" title.
2. To move a screen up or down within its system, drag it by the dotted handle at the start of its row.
3. To move a whole system above or below another, drag the system block by the dotted handle on its header.
   Fold a block first with its arrow if you want a shorter drag.
4. To remove a pin, click the red **✕** on its row.
   When a system's last screen is removed, the whole block disappears.
5. Click **Save**.
   Save stays greyed out until you have actually changed something.

If you try to leave with unsaved changes, the system asks whether to discard them or keep editing.
Screens cannot be dragged from one system into another - each screen belongs to its own system.

### Jump back to recent work

- The **Continue where you left off** row lists the screens you opened most recently, newest first.
- Click any of them to return to that screen.
- The row fills up as you work; it is remembered per person on this computer.

### Find a screen's user guide

- The **Help & guides** section lists your screens that have a built-in guide.
- Click one to open that screen, then click the **book** icon in the top bar to read the guide beside your work.

## Tips & troubleshooting

- Your pinned screens are saved to your sign-in, so they appear on any device you use.
  The view choices (hidden section, folded groups) and the recent-screens row are remembered per computer instead.
- If a pinned or recent screen no longer appears, your role's access to it was probably changed.
  The page only ever shows screens you can currently open - nothing is broken.
- If Quick access says "Nothing pinned yet", open the screens you use daily and click the star next to each title.
- The order you see on My Dashboard is exactly the order in the Manage window: systems top to bottom, then screens within each system.

## Related options

- Every screen's title carries the **star** used for pinning.
- Each system also has its own landing page (via the apps switcher in the top bar) listing all screens of that system - Quick access is your personal shortlist, the landing page is the full catalogue.
